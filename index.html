Call to Action

Chances are, you (like us) have been fighting against COVID-19.

The challenge before us is complex, important, and highly time-sensitive, which is why we've built a growing, interdisciplinary community of visionaries to form ventures responding to COVID-19.

The COVID-19 Response Innovation Lab, in affiliation with Stanford BioSecurity, is an accelerator program to help launch and grow promising projects within our Stanford community.

We are starting this initiative collaboratively – it's completely new and has both exciting momentum and huge potential, which is why we would love for you to join the effort. Since its inception a few days ago, over 200 members have joined and over 150 have signed up to be placed into venture formation teams with considerable time to dedicate. Our membership began with the incoming GSB MBA class of 2022 and now includes a balance of members from the Stanford Graduate School of Business, School of Engineering, and School of Medicine.

We feel that using innovation to help systems, structures, and societies that are ill-prepared to deal with this pandemic is the best way to help in this time. From professors to students, many of us are sitting at home, eager to contribute with our diverse and important backgrounds and skillsets. We want to support the many great ideas we are seeing. We want to help you realize them into solutions and get them on the street – maximal, rapid positive impact!

From workforce allocation to mental health to therapeutics and vaccine proposals, solutions focused on issues ranging from immediate impact to recovery to long-term preparation, we are organized around different challenges that encompass both existing projects and new ideas across multiple cities, counties, and continents.

Let's save the world together.
