mPCR (mobile PCR) Viral Diagnostic Device

Team Lead: Dr. Demir Akin

Team Contact: demir.akin@stanford.edu

Current Team: Garand Tyson, Frank Zheng, Valeria Wu

Description: Extremely affordable, scalable testing (DNA/RNA sequencing).

We are leading an international innovative research project with the vision to build a smartphone app that can be used by anyone in the world (at home or in the hospital). It will use deep learning to determine whether they need to get further testing for COVID-19 based on audio/visual signals (e.g. cough sound, lung noise, video of face) + textual data (questionnaires). Did you know that videos of the face can be used to determine heart rate, blood oxygen level, respiratory rate, and stress levels?

More details / proposal here.

We have Pakistan universities/hospital/government on board to gather data at hospitals shortly. Also our group has linkage to Japanese government and the Prime Minister level in the UK/Netherlands.

We don't know if this ground-breaking research will succeed and don't know of anybody else working on it. If it works, we hope to share the system freely worldwide to help end this pandemic! Perhaps the app usage requirement is a promise to never again be hostile to another nation.

Looking for: We need to replicate the existing devices or finalize the manufacturing ot the version 2 device. For this purpose we need tema members with experise in the following areas. People with:

  • EE background: hands-on PCB design, fabrication and circuit design experience, people who know and can directly communicate with PCB fabrication facilities vendors that have pick-and-place capability AND can offer their service free or donate/discount their servies for a non-profit cause

  • analog an digital signal processing experience, especially with exp with microcontrollers, Arduino, or similar microcontrollers

  • 3D printing experince to fabricate a case for the device so it can be safely distributed to the public

  • Android/iOS App/GUI developers

  • business backgrounds, people who have previously been involved in start up creation, have connections to or willing to connect with angel investors, ventures/foundations etc., people with social media, and outreach experience

  • people with RT-PCR and molecular biology expertise to run the inital validation tests with simulants (not clincal samples)

  • people with BSL-3 lab experince and access to such contolled regulation labs for perfoming the validation testing with clinical samples (this need will come later on but forming a team from get-go is critical for rapid respnse to this pandemic)

Intranasal personalized antibody spray (CRISPR-Cas13 targeting of SARS-COV-2)

Team Lead: Jordan Strasser

Team Contact: j217@stanford.edu

Current Team: Mandeep Singh

Description: Taking advantage of COVID-19's long incubation period. Design CRISPR-Cas13 system targetted to key genetic sequences of S-protein, Orf1ab and RDRP. Deliver system in polystyrene nanoparticle vessel. Have asymptomatic people do intranasal sprays, to essentially nip the virus in the bud. Whether someone actually has the virus or not shouldn't matter, as the target sequences are known through well-established PCR protocols to be virus-specific.
